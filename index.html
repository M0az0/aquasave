Why It Matters

Water Is Not a Privilege.
It's a Human Right.

The global water crisis is silent, pervasive, and entirely solvable. Here's what the numbers reveal — and why AquaSave exists.

☠️
829,000

Deaths Per Year

People die annually from diseases caused by unsafe water, poor sanitation, and lack of hygiene — that's one person every 38 seconds.

Source: WHO, 2023
👧
50%

Of Schools Lack Safe Water

Half of all schools globally have no clean drinking water — forcing children, especially girls, to drop out or risk their health daily.

Source: UNICEF, 2023
⏱️
200M

Hours Lost Daily

Women and girls spend 200 million hours every day collecting water — time stolen from education, work, and family.

Source: UN Water, 2022
💰

Annual Economic Loss

The global economy loses over $260 billion yearly due to lack of basic water and sanitation — affecting GDP in low-income nations most.

Source: World Bank, 2023

The System

Smart Water Intelligence,
End to End

A four-stage intelligent pipeline — detecting, analyzing, alerting, and resolving water threats before communities are harmed.

📡
01

Sensors Collect Data

IoT sensors measure pH, turbidity, pathogens, and temperature across wells and pipelines — transmitting data every 60 seconds.

→
🧠
02

AI Analyzes Quality

Machine learning models detect anomalies against WHO thresholds, predicting contamination events before they spread downstream.

→
🔔
03

Alerts Are Sent

Instant multi-channel alerts — SMS, email, dashboard — dispatched simultaneously to operators, ministries, and field teams.

→
🏛️
04

Authorities Respond

Geo-tagged incident reports guide response teams. Every event feeds back into the AI model, sharpening future predictions.

⚙️ This pipeline is a simulated architecture based on existing IoT and ML frameworks.
Solutions

Five Pillars of
Clean Water Action

Each solution targets a different root cause of the global water crisis — working together as a unified system.

01

Smart Water Intelligence for a Sustainable Future 💧

IoT-powered sensor networks deliver real-time quality data — detecting contaminants before they reach homes, schools, or hospitals.

  • pH, turbidity & pathogen sensing
  • Live dashboard for governments
  • Predictive maintenance alerts
02

Community Rainwater Harvesting Systems 🌧️

Modular, low-cost rainwater capture units designed to climate patterns — reducing groundwater dependency while creating local jobs.

  • Serves up to 500 households
  • Solar-powered filtration
  • Community ownership model
03

Sanitation Infrastructure Grants 🏗️

Micro-grants to fund hygienic toilet construction, wastewater treatment, and hygiene education — prioritising schools and clinics first.

  • Grants $2,000 – $50,000
  • Schools & clinics priority
  • Transparent impact reporting
04

Open Water Equity Index 📊

A free, open-access dataset mapping water access gaps across nations — arming policymakers and journalists with the evidence for change.

  • Multi-country dataset
  • Free public API access
  • Policy brief library
05

Youth Water Literacy Programs 🎓

School curricula, teacher training kits, and youth ambassador networks — because long-term change begins with the next generation.

  • Curriculum in 18 languages
  • Teacher training network
  • 34-country ambassador program

Pilot Simulation

Case Study: Egypt 🇪🇬

A simulated deployment scenario modelling AquaSave in a peri-urban community on the outskirts of Cairo — showing potential before/after outcomes.

📍 Context (Simulated)

Before AquaSave

  • ❌ No water quality monitoring
  • ❌ Manual testing every 3–4 weeks
  • ❌ Contamination detected days late
  • ❌ 68% using unverified water sources
  • ❌ High waterborne illness in children
⚙️ Intervention Applied

AquaSave Solution

  • ✅ 12 IoT sensor nodes deployed
  • ✅ Real-time dashboard for authority
  • ✅ SMS alerts for 3 response teams
  • ✅ 40 hygiene ambassadors trained
  • ✅ Rainwater unit at central school
📊 Projected Outcomes (Simulation)

After 12 Months

  • 📈 Detection: 3 weeks → 4 hours
  • 📈 Safe access: 32% → 81%
  • 📈 Response time: −75%
  • 📈 Water literacy: +60%
  • 📈 ~1,200 illness cases avoided
⚠️ Projected estimates from simulation — not verified field data.
