Our Foundation

Clean Water &
Sanitation for All

United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 6 calls on the world to ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all people by 2030. AquaSave was born to accelerate that vision.

Water scarcity affects more than 40% of the global population. Open defecation, contaminated rivers, and crumbling infrastructure claim hundreds of thousands of lives each year — disproportionately those of women and children in the developing world. We build technology-driven, community-led solutions that close this gap permanently.

Our Solutions

How AquaSave
Makes Change

Three pillars of action — designed to deliver measurable results in communities that need it most.

01

Smart Water Monitoring

IoT-powered sensor networks deployed in watersheds, wells, and distribution systems deliver real-time quality data — detecting contaminants before they reach homes. Alerts go out instantly to local operators and health authorities, cutting response time by up to 80%.

  • pH, turbidity & pathogen sensing
  • Dashboard for local governments
  • Predictive maintenance alerts
02

Community Rainwater Harvesting

We design and fund low-cost modular rainwater capture systems tailored to local climate patterns. Each system serves up to 500 households and reduces dependency on overdrawn groundwater by 60% while creating local maintenance jobs.

  • Modular rooftop collection units
  • Solar-powered filtration
  • Community training & ownership
03

Sanitation Infrastructure Grants

Our micro-grant program partners with local NGOs and municipal governments to fund hygienic toilet construction, wastewater treatment, and public hygiene education — prioritising schools and healthcare facilities first.

  • Grants from $2 000 – $50 000
  • Priority for schools & clinics
  • Transparent impact reporting
04

Data-Driven Advocacy

Our open-access Water Equity Index maps access gaps across 120+ countries, giving policymakers, journalists, and civil society the evidence base they need to drive legislative change and attract investment to under-served regions.

  • 120-country dataset, updated quarterly
  • Free public API
  • Policy brief library
05

Youth Education Programs

We equip the next generation with water literacy — in-school curriculum, teacher training kits, and a youth ambassador network in 34 countries. When children understand water, entire households change their habits.

  • Curriculum in 18 languages
  • 3 400+ trained teachers
  • Youth ambassador network
