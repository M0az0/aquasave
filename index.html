The Human Reality

The Water Crisis
is Happening Now

Behind every statistic is a human being. In Egypt's Western Desert and across sub-Saharan Africa, water is not a given — it's a daily struggle.

In parts of rural sub-Saharan Africa and Egypt's western desert communities, women and children walk an average of 6 kilometres daily to collect water — water that is often contaminated. Every 2 minutes, a child under 5 dies from a water-related disease. This is not inevitable. It is entirely preventable.

Turn Off the Tap

Turn off the tap while brushing teeth or washing hands. A running tap wastes 12 litres per minute — pure waste.

💧 Saves ~24L/day

💡 Use a glass for rinsing to save even more

○
🔧

Fix Leaks Fast

A single dripping faucet wastes over 3,000 litres per year. A running toilet can waste 200 litres a day unnoticed.

💧 Saves ~8L/day

💡 Put food dye in tank — blue in bowl means leak

○
🪣

Bucket Over Hose

A garden hose uses 1,000 litres per hour. A bucket uses 10–20 litres total. Same result — 98% less water.

💧 Saves ~150L/wash

💡 Wash at dusk to prevent immediate evaporation

○
⏱️

Shorter Showers

Cutting your shower from 10 to 5 minutes saves ~45 litres each time. Over a year — that's 16,425 litres.

💧 Saves ~45L/shower

💡 Set a 5-minute timer and turn off while soaping

○
🌙

Water Plants at Night

Watering in early morning or evening reduces evaporation by up to 70%. Hot afternoon sun evaporates before roots can absorb.

💧 Saves ~30L/day

💡 Collect rainwater for free, zero-carbon irrigation

○
♻️

Reuse Cooking Water

Water from boiling pasta or vegetables is nutrient-rich. Cool it and use it to water houseplants — waste nothing.

💧 Saves ~5L/meal

💡 Pasta water also enhances sauce flavour

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked
Questions

Everything you need to know about water conservation and SDG 6.

What is UN SDG 6 and why does it matter?

UN Sustainable Development Goal 6 aims to ensure availability and sustainable management of clean water and sanitation for all by 2030. It matters because 2.2 billion people currently lack access to safe drinking water — causing preventable deaths, disease, and economic loss every single day.

How accurate is the water calculator?

The calculator uses internationally recognised per-activity water consumption averages from the WHO and Water Footprint Network. Results are estimates — real usage varies by appliance efficiency, local water pressure, and individual habits. Use it as a directional guide, not an exact measurement.

What does "virtual water" mean?

Virtual water (or "embedded water") is the water used to produce a good or service that you don't see directly. For example, 2,700 litres are used to grow the cotton in a single t-shirt. Understanding virtual water helps us make smarter choices beyond just the tap.

Where is my data stored?

All your data — habit scores, calculator results, and pledges — is stored entirely in your browser's localStorage. Nothing is sent to any server. You own your data. You can clear it at any time using the "Clear My Data" button in the calculator section.

Is AquaSave a real organisation?

AquaSave is a conceptual prototype built for educational purposes, aligned with UN SDG 6. All statistics are drawn from real sources (WHO, UNICEF, World Bank, UN) but the impact numbers and user counts shown on the platform are simulated estimates for demonstration.

How can I actually help with the water crisis?

Start with the 6 habits in our tracker — small actions compound over time. Beyond that: support organisations like Water.org, choose plant-based meals more often, fix household leaks immediately, and raise awareness by sharing your footprint card with others.
