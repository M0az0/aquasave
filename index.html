SDG 6
Our Foundation

Clean Water &
Sanitation for All

United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 6 calls on the world to ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all people by 2030.

Water scarcity affects more than 40% of the global population. Open defecation, contaminated rivers, and crumbling infrastructure claim hundreds of thousands of lives each year. AquaSave is a prototype demonstrating how technology-driven, community-led solutions could close this gap permanently.

The System

How AquaSave Works

A four-stage pipeline — from sensor to solution — designed to detect, analyze, alert, and resolve water quality threats.

📡
01

Sensors Collect Data

IoT sensors deployed across wells, pipelines, and watersheds continuously measure pH, turbidity, temperature, and pathogen indicators every 60 seconds.

→
🧠
02

AI Analyzes Quality

A machine learning model processes incoming readings, detects anomalies, and compares against WHO safety thresholds — predicting contamination before it spreads.

→
🔔
03

Alerts Are Sent

When a threshold is breached, instant SMS, email, and dashboard alerts are dispatched to local operators, health ministries, and community coordinators.

→
🏛️
04

Authorities Respond

Response teams are guided by geo-tagged incident reports. Post-incident data feeds back into the model, improving future detection accuracy.

⚙️ This pipeline is a simulated architecture based on existing IoT and ML frameworks.
Our Solutions

How AquaSave
Makes Change

Three pillars of action — designed to deliver measurable results.

01

Smart Water Monitoring

IoT-powered sensor networks deliver real-time quality data — detecting contaminants before they reach homes.

  • pH, turbidity & pathogen sensing
  • Dashboard for local governments
  • Predictive maintenance alerts
02

Community Rainwater Harvesting

Low-cost modular rainwater capture systems tailored to local climate patterns, serving communities sustainably.

  • Modular rooftop collection units
  • Solar-powered filtration
  • Community training & ownership
03

Sanitation Infrastructure Grants

A micro-grant program partnering with local NGOs and municipalities to fund hygienic toilet construction and education.

  • Grants from $2,000 – $50,000
  • Priority for schools & clinics
  • Transparent impact reporting
04

Data-Driven Advocacy

An open-access Water Equity Index mapping access gaps across countries, giving policymakers evidence to drive change.

  • Multi-country dataset
  • Free public API
  • Policy brief library
05

Youth Education Programs

Equipping the next generation with water literacy through curriculum, teacher training, and ambassador networks.

  • Curriculum in multiple languages
  • Teacher training network
  • Youth ambassador program

Pilot Simulation

Case Study: Egypt 🇪🇬

A simulated deployment scenario modelling how AquaSave could operate in a peri-urban community on the outskirts of Cairo.

📍 Context (Simulated)

Before AquaSave

  • ❌ No water quality monitoring infrastructure
  • ❌ Manual testing every 3–4 weeks only
  • ❌ Contamination detected days after exposure
  • ❌ 68% using unverified water sources
  • ❌ High waterborne illness in children under 5
⚙️ Solution Applied

AquaSave Intervention

  • ✅ 12 IoT sensor nodes deployed across the network
  • ✅ Real-time dashboard for local water authority
  • ✅ SMS alert system for 3 response teams
  • ✅ Training for 40 hygiene ambassadors
  • ✅ Rainwater harvesting unit at central school
📊 Projected Outcomes (Simulation)

After 12 Months

  • 📈 Detection time: 3 weeks → 4 hours
  • 📈 Safe water access: 32% → 81%
  • 📈 Response time reduced by ~75%
  • 📈 Community water literacy: +60%
  • 📈 ~1,200 illness cases potentially avoided
⚠️ All outcomes are projected estimates from simulation — not verified field data.
