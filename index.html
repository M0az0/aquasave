The Human Reality

The Water Crisis
is Happening Now

Behind every statistic is a human being. In Egypt's own Western Desert communities and across sub-Saharan Africa, water is not a given — it's a daily struggle.

In parts of rural sub-Saharan Africa and Egypt's western desert communities, women and children walk an average of 6 kilometres daily to collect water — water that is often contaminated. Every 2 minutes, a child under 5 dies from a water-related disease. This is not inevitable. It is entirely preventable.

Compiled from WHO, UNICEF & UN Water Reports, 2023

Turn Off the Tap
Turn off the tap while brushing teeth or washing hands. A running tap wastes 12 litres per minute — pure waste.
💧 Saves ~24L/day
💡 Tip: Use a glass for rinsing to save even more
○
🔧
Fix Leaks Fast
A single dripping faucet wastes over 3,000 litres per year. A running toilet can waste 200 litres a day unnoticed.
💧 Saves ~8L/day
💡 Tip: Put food dye in tank — blue in bowl means leak
○
🪣
Bucket Over Hose
A garden hose uses 1,000 litres per hour. A bucket uses 10–20 litres total. Same clean car, 98% less water.
💧 Saves ~150L/wash
💡 Tip: Wash at dusk to prevent immediate evaporation
○
⏱️
Shorter Showers
Cutting your shower from 10 to 5 minutes saves approximately 45 litres each time. Over a year — that's 16,425 litres.
💧 Saves ~45L/shower
💡 Tip: Set a 5-minute timer — turn off water while soaping
○
🌙
Water at Night
Watering plants in early morning or evening reduces evaporation by up to 70%. Hot afternoon sun evaporates before roots absorb.
💧 Saves ~30L/day
💡 Tip: Collect rainwater for free irrigation
○
♻️
Reuse Cooking Water
Water used to boil pasta, vegetables, or eggs is full of nutrients. Let it cool and use it to water houseplants — waste nothing.
💧 Saves ~5L/meal
💡 Tip: Also reuse pasta water in sauces for extra flavour
